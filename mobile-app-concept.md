# TaskStack Mobile: The "Anti-Hustle" Companion

## App Description

TaskStack Mobile is not just another aggressively demanding to-do app that pushes you to burnout—it is a Slow Productivity OS built entirely for your pocket. In a world of endless notifications and toxic hustle culture, TaskStack acts as your personal anchor, focusing on skill mastery, energy management, and deep work rather than meaningless checkboxes.

Designed with a sleek, minimalist interface, the app brings the power of AI-driven career roadmaps and "anti-hustle" philosophy wherever you go. Whether you're a junior developer trying to learn a new framework on a train commute or a senior designer logging sudden bursts of creative energy, TaskStack Mobile syncs your life seamlessly. With an integrated Energy Mapper, a Pomodoro timer built for focus (and breaks!), and a Distraction Journal to offload mental clutter, the app ensures you only work when you're at your best. 

It is the app that actually tells you to put the phone down and take a nap.

---

## Extra Mobile-Native Feature Suggestions

### 1. **OS-Level Health & Sleep Integration**
Integrate with Apple HealthKit or Google Fit. If the app detects you only got 4 hours of sleep, it automatically adjusts your "Energy Mapper" for the day, dialing down your AI-generated tasks to lighter learning modules instead of deep, focus-heavy coding challenges.

### 2. **Context-Aware Focus Modes (Location & Bluetooth)**
Use location services or Bluetooth triggers (like connecting to a specific pair of noise-canceling headphones or arriving at your favorite coffee shop) to automatically launch the Pomodoro timer and mute non-essential notifications via Apple's Focus filters.

### 3. **Interactive Lock Screen & Home Screen Widgets**
- **The "Vibe Check" Widget:** A tiny widget to quickly log your current energy level (High, Medium, Crash) without opening the app.
- **Micro-Learning Widget:** Displays a bite-sized snippet or flashcard from your active AI roadmap for quick consumption while waiting in line.
- **Standby Mode / Dynamic Island:** Keep the Pomodoro countdown visible in the iPhone Dynamic Island or on the bedside Standby screen.

### 4. **Voice-to-Text Distraction Journal**
Sometimes an idea hits you while you’re walking or driving. Use native speech-to-text to let users quickly "dump" a thought into the Distraction Journal by just tapping a record button or using a Siri/Google Assistant shortcut ("Hey Siri, log a distraction in TaskStack").

### 5. **"Do Not Disturb" Zen Mode**
When a Pomodoro timer starts, the app can utilize native screen-time APIs to softly block or delay access to social media apps (like Twitter, Instagram, or TikTok) until the focus session is complete. 

### 6. **Offline First with Background Sync**
Commutes often mean spotty cellular service. The mobile app should cache your active mastery track and current tasks offline, locally queueing any progress or journal entries, and automatically background-syncing with Redis/Vercel once you are back on Wi-Fi.

### 7. **Haptic Feedback for Slow Productivity**
Use the Taptic Engine to reinforce "Slow Productivity" rules. A gentle, rhythmic heartbeat vibration when entering "Deep Work" mode, or a distinct, calming haptic chime when it's time to step away from the phone and rest.

### 8. **NFC Tag Support**
Allow users to write to cheap NFC tags. Stick an NFC tag to a notebook, physical desk, or even a water bottle. Tapping the phone to the tag instantly logs a specific action (e.g., "Started Deep Work", "Drank Water", "Energy Restored").

### 9. **Push Notifications (That Aren't Annoying)**
Instead of pinging the user because they *haven't* done a task, the AI sends contextual check-ins: "You've been in High Energy mode for 3 hours. It's mathematically time for a break" or "You mastered 3 React concepts this week, let's just review today."
